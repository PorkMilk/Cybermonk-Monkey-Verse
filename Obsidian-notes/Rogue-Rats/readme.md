

# Rogue Rats — General

## Overview
Subterranean storm drains and sewer pipes beneath a Central Texas suburban neighborhood. Dark, gritty, industrial, dystopian. Inspired by the *Vampire: The Masquerade* Nosferatu archetype — grimy, plague-ridden, unsettling, highly detailed.

**Core Protocol (Absolute Concealment):** The rats are never seen by surface dwellers. They exist only in the shadows of the subterranean infrastructure.

**Tone:** Vicious, deceitful, internally competitive, paranoid. The rats view the surface world as a theater of operations to be audited from the shadows.

## Physiology & Appearance
- Wild, plague-ridden rats — scarred, diseased, unkempt
- Visible sores, matted, dirty fur
- Anthropomorphic: stand on hind legs, forelimbs used as hands with human-like dexterity to manipulate scavenged tools/gear

## Culture & Behavior
- Skulking and vicious
- Prone to infighting over resources
- Bound by a rigid, desperate pact of silence regarding the surface

## Hierarchy
Modeled after state-level intelligence agencies (CIA, KGB, MI6):

| Role | Designation | Function |
|---|---|---|
| Leader | [[The Archivist]] | Central intelligence processing; oversees the underground network |
| Second-in-Command | [[The Handler]] | Oversees field cells; ensures deniability, enforces security |
| Intel Officer | [[The Cipher]] | Decrypts surface-world activity (monitors squirrel/cat interactions) |
| Field Operative | [[The Skulk]] | Deep-cover agents; observation + drainage-grate infiltration |

## Tactical Operations
- **The Network** — vast, unnamed comms infrastructure buried in the pipe system, relays info across neighborhoods
- **The Peek** — primary tactical maneuver; operatives stay in darkness, use storm drains as observation ports to audit surface activity without detection
- **Black-Out** — emergency defense protocol; when a surface threat approaches an observation point, all agents instantly freeze and submerge into dark water
